The Issue

Child labor is a sad reality that affects millions of children worldwide. It is important to understand the causes, consequences, and what can be done to eradicate this practice.

According to the ILO (International Labor Organization), more than 160 million children are involved in child labor, many of them in hazardous conditions. This impairs their physical and mental development and violates their basic rights.

Stories

Eliseu Alves

Eliseu Alves started working at seven years old, collecting trash and, at eight, selling candy on the street, which impacted his education, delaying his literacy until he was 12. Despite the challenges and traumas, such as police violence, he managed to return to school and, at 23, is proud to have helped support his younger siblings.

Marco Bastos

Marco Bastos, on the other hand, left school at 12 to work as a construction helper, maturing prematurely by living among adults. At 18, he found a better path by becoming an apprentice, but believes that no child should go through what he experienced, questioning how he could focus on school knowing he had to work afterward.

How to Help

There are several ways to contribute to the eradication of child labor. You can report it, volunteer, or donate to support the fight against this practice.

Report: If you know of cases of child labor, do not hesitate to report them to the relevant authorities.

Become a Volunteer: Participate in our awareness campaigns and help spread information about the dangers of child labor.

Donate Now: Contribute financially to support our actions against child labor, such as the work done by organizations like Fundação Telefônica Vivo and ChildFund Brazil, which focus on prevention and combatting child labor.
